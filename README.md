# GM-CNF: Condition-Invariant Semi-Supervised Fault Diagnosis under Time-Varying Conditions

Abstract: Intelligent fault diagnosis models face the dual challenges of label scarcity and severe operating condition variations in practical industrial applications. Nonlinear fluctuations in rotational speed and load severely distort low-dimensional data manifolds, rendering traditional semi-supervised learning (SSL) methods highly susceptible to negative feature transfer and confirmation bias. To address these issues, this paper proposes a Gaussian Mixture Conditional Normalizing Flow (GM-CNF) framework. To tackle the manifold distortion induced by drastic condition changes, the framework explicitly injects physical condition priors into an invertible flow network. By actively counteracting nonlinear disturbances, it achieves the analytical decoupling of fault semantics from condition variables, thereby extracting pure, condition-invariant representations. By utilizing a minimal amount of labeled data to precisely anchor Gaussian cluster centers, the proposed model performs probabilistic soft assignment through unsupervised marginal likelihood maximization, effectively guiding massive unlabeled features to converge toward predefined anchors. Furthermore, benefiting from the exact probability density estimation inherent in normalizing flows, the proposed method naturally enables high-precision rejection of unknown faults without requiring any architectural modifications. Extensive experiments on five real-world datasets with time-varying conditions demonstrate that the proposed method not only outperforms existing baselines in closed-set diagnostic accuracy under extremely low label rates but also exhibits robust open-set detection capabilities for unknown classes, providing an effective approach for highly reliable industrial monitoring.
